Projects & Analysis

The following projects were completed as part of my Master of Science in Applied Data Science program. They span machine learning, database management, financial analysis, and cloud infrastructure — each grounded in real-world business problems.

01

Predictive Analytics — Heart Disease Detection

Machine Learning · Advanced

Using a CDC dataset of over 300,000 patient questionnaire responses, a predictive model was built to help medical professionals identify patients at high risk for heart disease. Six classifiers were evaluated using a 5-fold cross-validation pipeline, with Logistic Regression emerging as the top performer at an F1-Macro score of 0.76 and ROC-AUC of 0.84. Key findings revealed that age and chronic conditions such as stroke, kidney disease, and diabetes are the strongest predictors, while sleep, exercise, BMI, and mental health represent measurable levers for early intervention.

02

SQL Querying for Mortgage Lender

Database Management · Intermediate

Designed and implemented a relational SQL database in Azure for First Rate Financial, a Texas mortgage brokerage funding over $100 million annually. The database separates data across Property, Borrower, and Loan tables, enabling Loan Officers to identify refinance opportunities among past clients and evaluate new applicants by risk level. Borrowers were segmented into High, Medium, and Low Risk groups using income, debt-to-income ratio, FICO scores, and loan-to-value — improving operational efficiency across the lending workflow.

SQL Azure Database Design Risk Segmentation Mortgage Analytics
03

FAANG Stock Data Analysis

Scripting for Data Analysis · Intermediate

Analyzed the performance of five major tech stocks — NVDA, AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL, and AMZN — against the S&P 500 and DOW over a 15-year period. The project employed time series analysis, correlation studies, and sentiment analysis using the Tweepy API to measure market volatility in relation to major news events. Key finding: NVIDIA showed the lowest correlation with other FAANG stocks, while Apple, Google, Amazon, and Microsoft moved closely in tandem with broader market benchmarks.

Python Time Series Sentiment Analysis yfinance API Correlation Analysis
04

Cloud Migration Feasibility Study

Cloud Management · Intermediate

Conducted a comprehensive cloud migration feasibility study for a fictional music streaming platform (Cecilia) competing against Spotify and Apple Music. AWS, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud were evaluated across 19 categories spanning financial, technical, and operational dimensions. The cost analysis revealed on-premises operations running $13.1M annually versus an AWS equivalent of $2.4–$3.1M — generating estimated savings of $9.6M–$10.4M per year and over $115 million across a 10-year horizon. AWS was identified as the clear choice for media-specific workloads.
